Facts About Brain Stroke

What is a Stroke?

A stroke, also known as a cerebrovascular accident (CVA), occurs when there is a sudden interruption in the blood supply to the brain. This can cause brain cells to die, leading to loss of function in the affected areas.

Types of Stroke

  • Ischemic Stroke: Caused by a blockage in a blood vessel supplying blood to the brain.
  • Hemorrhagic Stroke: Caused by bleeding in or around the brain due to a ruptured blood vessel.
  • Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA): A "mini-stroke" that signals an increased risk of future stroke.

Risk Factors

  • High Blood Pressure: The leading risk factor for stroke.
  • Smoking: Increases the risk of stroke by damaging blood vessels.
  • Diabetes: Contributes to plaque buildup in blood vessels.
  • High Cholesterol: Leads to plaque formation, increasing stroke risk.
  • Obesity: Increases risk through high blood pressure, diabetes, and cholesterol.
  • Family History: A family history of stroke can increase your risk.

Symptoms of Stroke

  • Sudden Numbness or Weakness: Especially on one side of the body.
  • Confusion or Trouble Speaking: Difficulty understanding speech or slurred speech.
  • Vision Problems: Sudden trouble seeing in one or both eyes.
  • Difficulty Walking: Loss of balance, coordination, or sudden dizziness.
  • Severe Headache: A sudden, severe headache with no known cause.

Prevention

  • Healthy Diet: Helps manage cholesterol and blood pressure levels.
  • Regular Exercise: Maintains a healthy weight and lowers blood pressure.
  • Limit Alcohol Consumption: Reduces the risk of high blood pressure.
  • Regular Check-ups: Helps monitor and manage risk factors.

Treatment Options

  • Medications: Antiplatelet drugs, anticoagulants, and blood pressure medications.
  • Surgery: Procedures like endarterectomy or angioplasty.
  • Rehabilitation: Physical, occupational, and speech therapy after a stroke.

Stroke Recovery

Recovery varies based on severity and individual factors. Early treatment and rehabilitation improve recovery outcomes.

Statistical Insights

  • Stroke is the fifth leading cause of death in many countries.
  • About 15 million people worldwide experience a stroke annually, with 5 million dying and 5 million left permanently disabled.
